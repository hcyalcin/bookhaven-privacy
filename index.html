Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 27, 2026 · Türkçe

BookHaven does not collect, store, or transmit your personal data to any server we operate. Your library, reading progress, and preferences live on your device. If you choose to use iCloud sync, your data travels through your private Apple iCloud — not through us. We never see it.

What stays on your device

By default, all of the following data is stored locally on your device. None of it is sent to any server BookHaven operates:

  • Your book library and imported files
  • Reading progress, bookmarks, and highlights
  • Reading statistics and streaks
  • App preferences and settings
  • Listening history and audiobook progress

If iCloud sync is enabled (it is by default when you are signed into iCloud, and you can turn it off in Settings › iCloud Sync), some of this data also flows through your private iCloud so it can travel across your Apple devices — see the next section.

iCloud sync

When iCloud is available on your device and you have not turned sync off, BookHaven uses Apple's CloudKit framework to keep your reading state in step across the Apple devices you sign into with the same Apple ID. We have no servers in this loop. Your data is stored in your private iCloud database, which only you and your Apple devices can read; BookHaven never sees the contents.

Three tiers of data are handled differently:

  • Library, bookmarks, progress, shelves, reading history, sync-tier preferences — sync automatically when iCloud sync is on. This is the default.
  • Ebook files (EPUB, PDF, MOBI, AZW3, FB2, TXT, RTF) under 40 MB — sync only if you explicitly opt in via Settings › iCloud Sync › Sync Ebook Files. The toggle is off by default; you choose to turn it on per device. Files over 40 MB are kept local so iCloud transfers stay reliable.
  • Audiobook files — never sync via iCloud. Audiobooks stay on the device where you added them. If you also imported them from Dropbox, Google Drive, or OneDrive (see "Optional cloud import" below), you can re-add them on another device from the same source.

You can turn iCloud sync off entirely at any time via Settings › iCloud Sync. You can remove ebook files already synced to iCloud via the Remove Synced Files from iCloud action in the same settings panel. Per-book sync overrides (always sync this book / never sync this book) are available from each book's detail view. You can also export a complete local JSON backup of your library at any time from Settings — useful before switching Apple IDs or as a personal safety net.

Apple's iCloud is governed by Apple's Privacy Policy. BookHaven is not informed of and does not see the contents of your iCloud database.

Optional cloud import (Dropbox, Google Drive, OneDrive)

You can optionally connect a Dropbox, Google Drive, or OneDrive account to import books that already live there. We use OAuth 2.1 with PKCE to authenticate; the access token stays on your device's keychain and is never transmitted to any BookHaven server (we don't have one). Imports happen device-to-cloud-provider directly. If you sync your library to iCloud and then open BookHaven on another device, files that came from your cloud accounts can be re-downloaded from the same source on that device. Disconnecting an account in Settings › Cloud Accounts deletes its token from the keychain immediately.

Cover art lookup

When you import a book or browse cover art options, BookHaven may send the book title and author name to public APIs (Apple iTunes, Google Books, and Open Library) to find matching cover artwork. No personal information, device identifiers, or reading history is included in these requests.

Sample audiobook download

BookHaven offers a single optional sample download of A Christmas Carol from the Internet Archive (archive.org), a public LibriVox recording. Tapping the download button initiates a standard HTTPS file transfer. No account, identifier, or reading history is transmitted; the Internet Archive sees only the file request and your IP address, as it does for any visitor to their site.

Purchases

In-app purchases are handled entirely by Apple through StoreKit. BookHaven does not operate its own payment server and does not receive or store your payment information. Please refer to Apple's Privacy Policy for details on how Apple handles transaction data.

Advertising

After your seven-day welcome gift expires, BookHaven shows ads in transitional surfaces — brief banners on the Discover and Stats screens, and short interstitials between certain transitions (when you open a book, return to the app after being away for more than ten minutes, or move from your haven to Discover). Ads never appear during reading, audiobook playback, or in your haven, library, or relax mode. You can remove ads entirely at any time by subscribing to Ad-Free Membership from the Shop or Settings.

Ads are served by Google's AdMob service. AdMob is a third party; their handling of ad-request data is governed by Google's Privacy Policy. AdMob receives only the technical information it needs to serve and measure ads — for example, your device type, IP address, ad-request timestamp, coarse country-level location, and an Apple-managed identifier (the IDFA) only if you previously allowed tracking via the iOS dialog described below. AdMob does not receive your reading history, bookmarks, library contents, or any in-app behavior beyond the fact that an ad was requested.

Ad measurement (App Tracking Transparency)

The first time an ad is about to appear, BookHaven asks iOS to show the standard App Tracking Transparency dialog. This dialog asks whether ads in BookHaven can use limited cross-app measurement (an Apple-managed identifier called the IDFA) to gauge whether ads are working. Either choice is fine:

  • Allow: BookHaven and AdMob may use the IDFA to measure ad performance. The IDFA is an opaque, resettable identifier managed by iOS — you can reset or revoke it at any time in iOS Settings › Privacy & Security › Tracking.
  • Don't Allow: BookHaven and AdMob do not use the IDFA. Ads still appear, but measurement uses Apple's privacy-preserving SKAdNetwork instead. Ad revenue per impression may be lower; the experience is otherwise identical.

If you are in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, BookHaven additionally presents Google's User Messaging Platform consent form (required by EU and UK GDPR) before ads load on first launch. You can revisit your consent choice at any time via Settings › Privacy › Manage Ad Privacy Choices.

Rewarded ads (optional)

You can choose to watch two short rewarded videos to bank thirty minutes of ad-free reading. This is entirely optional and triggered only by your tap on Settings › Bank Quiet Time › Watch 2 videos now. The same data principles apply — AdMob serves the videos, the IDFA is used only if you previously allowed tracking, and your reading content is not shared.

No analytics or behavioral tracking

Beyond the ad-related data described above, BookHaven does not use any analytics services, crash reporting tools, or behavioral tracking. There are no cookies and no device fingerprinting outside the iOS-managed IDFA (when you allow it).

No account required

BookHaven does not require you to create an account, sign in, or provide any personal information to use the app.

Third-party services

BookHaven uses only the following third-party services, and contacts them only when the relevant feature is used:

  • Apple iCloud (CloudKit) — when iCloud sync is enabled, your library and reading state are stored in your private iCloud database (governed by Apple's privacy policy). BookHaven has no visibility into this data.
  • Apple StoreKit — for processing in-app purchases (governed by Apple's privacy policy)
  • Google AdMob — serves banner, interstitial, and rewarded ads on the free tier after your welcome gift expires. Receives technical ad-request information (device type, IP address, country, ad timestamp, IDFA when allowed via ATT). Governed by Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Google User Messaging Platform (UMP) — presents the GDPR consent form to users in the EEA, UK, or Switzerland on first launch. Free Google service used solely for legal consent collection.
  • Public book APIs — iTunes Search, Google Books, and Open Library for cover art only (book title and author sent, no personal data)
  • Dropbox, Google Drive, and Microsoft OneDrive — only if you explicitly connect an account. OAuth tokens stay on your device. We never proxy or store your provider data.
  • Internet Archive — only when you tap the optional "Sample audiobook" button to download a public LibriVox recording

Data retention

Local data on your device is controlled entirely by you. Reading progress, bookmarks, and imported books remain until you delete the individual book, reset the app, or delete BookHaven itself — which removes everything local. The keychain entry that records your trial start date is cleared when the app is deleted.

Data synced to your iCloud is controlled by you through the standard iOS data management tools. Deleting BookHaven from a device prompts iOS to ask whether you also want to delete its iCloud data; choosing yes removes the iCloud copy across all your Apple devices. You can also remove only the ebook files synced to iCloud (keeping metadata, bookmarks, and progress) via Settings › iCloud Sync › Remove Synced Files from iCloud.

Children's privacy

BookHaven does not knowingly collect any personal information from anyone, including children under the age of 13.
