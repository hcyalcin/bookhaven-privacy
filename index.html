Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 20, 2026

BookHaven does not collect, store, or transmit your personal data. Your library, reading progress, and preferences stay on your device.

What stays on your device

All of the following data is stored locally on your device and is never sent to any server:

  • Your book library and imported files
  • Reading progress, bookmarks, and highlights
  • Reading statistics and streaks
  • App preferences and settings
  • Listening history and audiobook progress

Cover art lookup

When you import a book or browse cover art options, BookHaven may send the book title and author name to public APIs (Apple iTunes, Google Books, and Open Library) to find matching cover artwork. No personal information, device identifiers, or reading history is included in these requests.

Sample audiobook download

BookHaven offers a single optional sample download of A Christmas Carol from the Internet Archive (archive.org), a public LibriVox recording. Tapping the download button initiates a standard HTTPS file transfer. No account, identifier, or reading history is transmitted; the Internet Archive sees only the file request and your IP address, as it does for any visitor to their site.

Purchases

In-app purchases are handled entirely by Apple through StoreKit. BookHaven does not operate its own payment server and does not receive or store your payment information. Please refer to Apple's Privacy Policy for details on how Apple handles transaction data.

No analytics or tracking

BookHaven does not use any analytics services, crash reporting tools, advertising SDKs, or tracking technologies. There are no cookies, no device fingerprinting, and no behavioral tracking of any kind.

No account required

BookHaven does not require you to create an account, sign in, or provide any personal information to use the app.

Third-party services

BookHaven uses only the following third-party services, and contacts them only when the relevant feature is used:

  • Apple StoreKit — for processing in-app purchases (governed by Apple's privacy policy)
  • Public book APIs — iTunes Search, Google Books, and Open Library for cover art only (book title and author sent, no personal data)
  • Internet Archive — only when you tap the optional "Sample audiobook" button to download a public LibriVox recording

Data retention

Because all of your data stays on your device, retention is controlled entirely by you. Reading progress, bookmarks, and imported books remain until you delete the individual book, reset the app, or delete BookHaven itself — which removes everything. The keychain entry that records your trial start date is cleared when the app is deleted.

Children's privacy

BookHaven does not knowingly collect any personal information from anyone, including children under the age of 13.

Open-source acknowledgments

BookHaven is built on open-source libraries, typefaces, and sound assets. Full attribution, license terms, and links to upstream projects are available in the app at Support › Open Source.

Future changes

BookHaven does not currently sync your library across devices. If iCloud sync is added in a future release, this policy will be updated to describe what data syncs through Apple's iCloud service (governed by Apple's privacy policy) and how to opt out. Sync, if added, will always be optional.

Contact

Questions about this policy or your data can be sent to support@bookhaven.app.

Changes to this policy

If this privacy policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a new "Last updated" date at the top. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
